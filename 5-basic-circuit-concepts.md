## Basic Circuit Concepts

In the next few paragraphs, we will discuss what electricity, charge, circuit, electrons, current, voltage, potential difference, and ohm’s law are.

You will encounter these concepts over and over again as we try to understand how Maya’s various electronics components work in greater detail.

Electricity is the flow of electric charge.

🎇 Animation of electric charge

Charge is a property of matter like mass or volume, it is measurable. It can be positive or negative. Electrons are particles that carry negative charges. Without it electricity, electronic gadget and robots like Maya won’t be able to work.

Electricity exists when electrons are able to move or constantly flow.

A circuit is an close loop where electrons can flow. Maya’s brain along with the various input and output components connected to it is a circuit.

🎇 Example of a circuit

Electrons flow because it will always move from a place with more negative charge to a place of less negative charge similar to how a ball will always fall from the top of a building to the ground.

🎇 Animation of a circuit

In technical terms, electrons move from a higher potential to a lower potential. The potential difference is the difference in charge between two point and is also called a voltage. The higher the voltage the faster the rates the electrons move. This rate is called the current.

🎇 Picture of analogy with water tank

The material in which elecrons can flow is a conductive material, the most common is a wire. In some materials elecrons can flow much better than in others the same way water can flow more easily in a wider pipe than in a narrow pipe. This is the concept of resistance.

🎇 Picture of water resistance

A narrow pipe “resists” the flow of water. There are electrical components (called resistors), which are mainly put on the circuit to do exactly just that by a certain controlled amount.

🎇 Picture of a resistor

That’s the concepts of current, voltage, and resistance.

Voltage is the difference in charge between two points.  Current is the rate at which the charges are flowing. Resistance is the tendency of the material to resist the flow of charge.

Voltage(V) is measured in volts. Current(I) measured in ampere. Resistance (R) in ohms. These are like how distance is measured in centimeters or inches. The relationship of current, voltage and resistance in mathematical terms is V = I*R (ohms law).

🎇 Picture of a circuit

The simple circuit above is a part of the circuit of Maya’s brain. That is a simple linear voltage regulator and the one encircled in pink is an LED (light emitting diode) connected to a current-limiting resistor.

The highest potential in Maya’s circuit is labeled at “RAW” (the highest voltage from Maya’s batteries), and the lowest potential is the “GND” or ground. Current flows from “RAW” to “GND”.

This is a basic circuit in which you can immediately apply ohm’s law. You can check Sparkfun’s Ohm’s law experiment to see Ohm’s law at work, and the circuit is very similar to almost exactly the same as the circuit encircled above.
